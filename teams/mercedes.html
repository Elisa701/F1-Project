Mercedes

Drivers: George Russell, Andrea Kimi Antonelli

Team Principal: Toto Wolff

Base: Brackley, England

Power Unit: Mercedes-AMG F1 Power Unit

History: Mercedes-AMG Petronas became one of the most dominant organizations Formula 1 has ever seen during the hybrid era. Although Mercedes has roots in Grand Prix racing dating back to the 1950s with Juan Manuel Fangio and Stirling Moss, the modern works team officially returned to Formula 1 in 2010 after acquiring Brawn GP. Under the leadership of Toto Wolff, Niki Lauda, and a world-class engineering structure, Mercedes transformed into the defining powerhouse of the turbo-hybrid era beginning in 2014. The team achieved an unprecedented run of eight consecutive Constructors’ Championships between 2014 and 2021 while Lewis Hamilton and Nico Rosberg secured seven straight Drivers’ Championships during that period. Mercedes became renowned for operational excellence, strategic consistency, engineering innovation, and one of the strongest technical departments in modern motorsport history. Following regulation changes and increased competition from Red Bull and McLaren, the team underwent a rebuilding phase before re-emerging as a title contender by 2025. Entering 2026, Mercedes combined its championship-winning experience with a new generation led by George Russell and Andrea Kimi Antonelli.
