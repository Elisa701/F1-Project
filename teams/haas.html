Haas

Drivers: Esteban Ocon, Oliver Bearman

Team Principal: Ayao Komatsu

Base: Kannapolis, USA

Power Unit: Ferrari

History: Haas F1 Team became Formula 1’s first American-led constructor in decades when it entered the championship in 2016 under businessman Gene Haas. Unlike traditional teams, Haas adopted a unique partnership-heavy model involving close technical cooperation with Ferrari, allowing the organization to compete efficiently despite relatively limited resources. The team achieved surprisingly competitive results during its early seasons and quickly established itself as a serious midfield competitor. Throughout its Formula 1 journey, Haas experienced periods of instability, leadership changes, and financial challenges, yet consistently remained one of the sport’s most resilient independent operations. Known for its straightforward and pragmatic approach, the team focused heavily on maximizing efficiency and driver development opportunities. By 2026, Haas continued building toward greater long-term competitiveness with a younger driver lineup and increasingly stable technical foundations.
