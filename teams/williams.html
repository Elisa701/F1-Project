Williams

Drivers: Carlos Sainz, Alexander Albon

Team Principal: James Vowles

Base: Grove, United Kingdom

Power Unit: Mercedes

History: Williams Racing is one of the most legendary independent teams in Formula 1 history. Founded by Sir Frank Williams and Patrick Head in 1977, the British team rose to prominence during the 1980s and 1990s, becoming one of the sport’s greatest engineering powerhouses. Williams secured multiple Constructors’ and Drivers’ Championships with iconic drivers including Nigel Mansell, Alain Prost, Nelson Piquet, Damon Hill, Jacques Villeneuve, and Keke Rosberg. During its peak years, the team became famous for technical innovation, professionalism, and a relentless competitive culture. After the departure of major manufacturers and changing financial realities in Formula 1, Williams experienced a prolonged decline throughout the 2010s. However, under new ownership by Dorilton Capital, the team entered a rebuilding phase centered on modernization, long-term investment, and renewed competitiveness. The arrival of experienced drivers such as Carlos Sainz Jr. alongside Alexander Albon signaled Williams’ ambition to return to the front of the grid. By 2026, the team was increasingly viewed as a resurgent force aiming to restore the prestige associated with one of Formula 1’s most historic names.
