Red Bull Racing

Drivers: Max Verstappen, Isack Hadjar,

Team Principal: Laurent Mekies

Base: Milton Keynes, United Kingdom

Power Unit: Honda RBPT

History: Red Bull Racing became one of Formula 1’s defining modern powerhouses after entering the sport in 2005 through the acquisition of Jaguar Racing. Backed by the global Red Bull brand, the team rapidly developed an aggressive and highly competitive identity centered on innovation, risk-taking, and elite driver development. Under Team Principal Christian Horner and legendary designer Adrian Newey, Red Bull dominated Formula 1 during the early 2010s with Sebastian Vettel before launching another historic era of success with Max Verstappen during the ground-effect regulations. Red Bull became particularly renowned for aerodynamic excellence, strategic aggression, and the strength of its junior driver academy, which produced multiple Formula 1 race winners and world champions. The organization’s ability to adapt quickly to regulation changes and maintain a winning culture established it as one of the benchmark teams of the modern era. By 2026, Red Bull remained one of the sport’s most feared competitors despite increasingly intense battles with McLaren, Ferrari, and Mercedes.
