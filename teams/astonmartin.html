Aston Martin

Drivers: Lance Stroll, Fernando Alonso

Team Principal: Jonathan Wheatley

Base: Silverstone, United Kingdom

Power Unit: Mercedes

History: Aston Martin’s modern Formula 1 project emerged through the transformation of Racing Point, which itself originated from the long-running Jordan Grand Prix organization founded in 1991. Over the decades, the team competed under several identities — including Midland, Spyker, Force India, and Racing Point — while consistently earning a reputation for efficiency and overachievement despite limited budgets. The rebranding to Aston Martin in 2021 marked the beginning of an ambitious manufacturer-backed project led by billionaire Lawrence Stroll. With major investments in infrastructure, a state-of-the-art factory, and the signing of high-profile figures such as Fernando Alonso and Adrian Newey, Aston Martin positioned itself as a future championship contender. The team became known for aggressive development strategies and long-term ambitions to challenge Formula 1’s traditional giants. By 2026, Aston Martin represented one of the fastest-growing projects in the sport and aimed to establish itself permanently among the leading teams.
