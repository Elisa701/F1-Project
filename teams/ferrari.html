Scuderia Ferrari

Drivers: Charles Leclerc, Lewis Hamilton

Team Principal: Frédéric Vasseur

Base: Maranello, Italy

Power Unit: Ferrari

History: Scuderia Ferrari is the oldest, most iconic, and most successful team in Formula 1 history, having competed in every single F1 season since the championship began in 1950. Founded by Enzo Ferrari, the Italian team became synonymous with passion, prestige, and motorsport excellence, building an unmatched legacy through legendary drivers such as Michael Schumacher, Niki Lauda, Gilles Villeneuve, Alberto Ascari, and Kimi Räikkönen. Ferrari’s golden eras include the dominant early 2000s period with Schumacher and Jean Todt, during which the team secured multiple Constructors’ and Drivers’ Championships in succession. Known worldwide for the unmistakable Rosso Corsa red cars and the emotional connection shared with the Tifosi, Ferrari represents far more than just a racing team — it is one of the most recognizable sporting institutions on the planet. Despite periods of inconsistency throughout the hybrid and ground-effect eras, Ferrari remained a constant front-running force and continued investing heavily in returning to championship-winning form. By 2026, with a driver lineup featuring Charles Leclerc and Lewis Hamilton, Ferrari entered a new era combining youth, experience, and enormous expectations from fans across the world.
