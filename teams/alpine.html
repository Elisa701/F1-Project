Alpine

Drivers: Pierre Gasly, Franco Colapinto

Team Principal: Flavio Briatore

Base: Enstone, United Kingdom

Power Unit: Renault

History: Alpine F1 Team represents the modern evolution of Renault’s long and complex Formula 1 history. The Enstone-based team originally entered Formula 1 as Toleman in the 1980s before becoming Benetton, where Michael Schumacher won his first two world championships in the mid-1990s. Renault later acquired the operation outright and achieved major success during the 2005 and 2006 seasons with Fernando Alonso, ending Ferrari’s era of dominance and securing consecutive Constructors’ and Drivers’ Championships. Rebranded as Alpine in 2021 to promote Renault’s performance-car division, the French manufacturer aimed to create a younger and more dynamic identity within Formula 1. Despite periods of inconsistency and management changes, Alpine continued investing heavily in infrastructure and long-term competitiveness. By 2026, the team remained focused on closing the gap to the leading organizations while balancing experienced leadership with emerging young talent.
